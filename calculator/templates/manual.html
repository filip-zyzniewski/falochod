GPX2Energy - calculate your EV power and energy needs

What is it?

This utility calculates power and energy requirements for a given commute using specified vehicle parameters.

How does it work?

It calculates momentary power requirements for each track point logged by the GPS receiver and aggregates them to provide the final results.

Forces taken into account:

  • Air drag
  • Rolling resistance
  • Force of incline

What do I need to do to make use of it?

Firstly you need to find out the parameters of your chosen donor car, these are a must:

  • Mass
  • Frontal area
  • Drag coefficient
  • possibly the Rolling resistance coefficient
You can get away with using default values for the rest for estimation purposes.

Secondly, you need to use another car to perform the commute you want to do with your EV and record it with a GPS receiver (I use My Tracks for Android).

Try to drive with lowest power level comfortable for you - if you get an electric motor according to the power specifications shown by this utility, then you won't be able to go much faster.

After logging the commute you need to save the logs as GPX files.

The last step is to set the vehicle parameters in the application form and upload the GPX files. In response you will get detailed power and energy statistics.
